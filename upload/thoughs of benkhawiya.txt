Historical-Cultural Context: Benkhawiya is a 21st-century African diasporic spiritual philosophy that draws from Nile Valley civilizations and Central/West African principles.
Four Lands Framework: A quadripartite cosmology (White, Black, Red, Green Lands) representing the structure of reality.
42 Ka Cube of Maat: A mathematical-theological foundation with 42 principles (41 active + 1 Divine Seer) across 7 dimensions.
Sacred Technologies: Mother Breath, iKhun Ritual System, Ben Stone Protocol, Law of Halces.
Developmental Ontology: Stages of human development and the 19 Reflections.
Community Cosmology: Temen as sacred space, Mer-based governance.
Theological Anthropology: Human as microcosm, Sa Ba Ka complex.
Eschatology and Cosmic Purpose: Individual and collective destiny.
Ethical Framework: Mer-Kunt dichotomy, Maat as foundation.
Comparative Theological Analysis: Parallels with African Traditional Religions, Kemetic theology, and diasporic innovation.
Contemporary Relevance: Addressing modern challenges.
Critical Theological Assessment: Strengths, challenges, and future development.

The previous framework (Amen, Nu, Sa, Ra) is a high-level cosmogony, while the Four Lands framework is a detailed cosmology of manifested reality. They can be integrated by seeing the Four Lands as the manifestation of the Amen-Nu-Sa-Ra process.
Distinction: The Amen-Nu-Sa-Ra process describes the emanation from unmanifest to manifest, while the Four Lands describe the structure of the manifest reality.
Developmental Path:
The 19 Reflections are consistent in both. The new exegesis adds more detail on stages (Vessel Preparation, etc.) which can be seen as elaborations of the 19 Reflections.
Divine Entities:
The Council of Four can be mapped to the Four Lands or to specific Ka principles. The Divine Seer might be seen as the culmination of the developmental path, which in the previous system was the Portal or Cosmic Collaborator.
Practices:
The practices in the new exegesis (Mother Breath, iKhun, etc.) are specific sacred technologies that can be incorporated into the daily practices (Morning Alignment, etc.) of the previous system.
Integration:

We can integrate the two by considering the new exegesis as the detailed theological and practical expansion of the Benkhawiya system, while the previous synthesis with Logos Cosmology provides the philosophical and metaphysical grounding.
